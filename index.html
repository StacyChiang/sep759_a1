Personal History Statement

JAN. 18, 2020 updated

I was born in rural Kaohsiung Taiwan, and so growing up I enjoyed exploring nature and various types of physical activity, including participating in the dance team and track team in elementary school. As the eldest daughter, I learned to like taking care of others and became someone with a sense of responsibility and consideration, whether with family, friends, or even pets. My parents are entrepreneurs, having started their own kindergarten from scratch when I was very young; seeing them working hard and dealing with sudden setbacks helped me learn to be pragmatic and always strive to seek solutions to any problems that I may face

My parents always supported me in making my own decisions and taking responsibility for the results. After graduating from junior high school, I chose to study at a vocational school rather than an ordinary senior high like the average Taiwanese student, hoping to learn and grow in a more diverse environment. After I graduated from there, my parents’ example led to me decide to study for a degree in business administration with a major in finance. Coming in without a formal background in business made the process challenging, but I persevered. Later, through an internship in the finance industry, I learned the importance of HR management to companies, resolving to learn more about the field in graduate school. During my graduate studies at Sun Yat-sen University, the all-English curriculum and my exchange to France opened my eyes to a wider world.

My first job upon entering the workforce was with Foxconn Technology Group, the world’s largest electronics component manufacturing company, as a senior specialist in the Corporate HR Division at the Group’s headquarters in Taiwan. In this role I was responsible for the training and recruitment development, including visiting the production line to understand the jobs of the various workstations. Thanks to my excellent performance and with a recommendation from my manager, I was transferred to the Apple product line to implement operation program management. Now I hope to pursue more advanced knowledge by returning to study, and in the future I will make use of what I will have learned to contribute to management in the technology manufacturing and production industry.
